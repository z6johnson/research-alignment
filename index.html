How rankings are calculated

Ranking Methodology

Faculty are ranked by an LLM-based analysis that evaluates alignment between each researcher's profile and the grant opportunity requirements. The system does not assign investigator roles — those decisions belong to the research team.

Scoring Dimensions

  • Expertise alignment — How closely the faculty member's stated research interests, keywords, and funded grant history match the grant's required expertise areas
  • Methodological fit — Whether the researcher's methods (as inferred from publications and grants) align with the methodological needs of the opportunity
  • Track record — Strength of the researcher's publication and funding history relative to the opportunity's scope and requirements

Data Sources

Faculty profiles are constructed from the university directory, enriched with data from NIH RePORTER (funded grants), PubMed (recent publications), ORCID (research outputs), and institutional profile pages. The composite score (0–100) reflects overall alignment strength, not a prediction of success.

Model

Grant requirements are extracted and faculty are matched using a large language model via LiteLLM. The model evaluates textual similarity and semantic relevance — it does not have access to private data, internal evaluations, or proprietary metrics. Results should be treated as a starting point for team assembly, not a definitive ranking.
